Steve Jobs

The man who wanted a computer looks like a book in 1978

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs's life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California
  • 1974 - Begins working as a game designer for Atari and develops the game - Super Breakout
  • 1976 - Founds the company Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak
  • 1976 - Launches first computer - the Apple I, which is made with wood and has a separate only keyboard and a screen
  • 1977 - Launches second computer - the Apple II, which become a huge success in the home computer market and make Apple a major player in the growing market
  • 1980 - Apple become a publicly traded company. In the same year, the Apple III release
  • 1983 - Apple release the technologically advanced Apple Lisa
  • 1984 - Apple introduce the Macintosh - first commercially viable computer with a graphical interface
  • 1985 - leaves Apple and starts new company - NeXT Computer
  • 1986 - buys Pixar from Lucasfilm founder George Lucas
  • 1996 - Apple buys NeXT for $402 million and thus get Jobs back
  • 1997 - becomes acting CEO at Apple
  • 2011 - dies at the age 56 in Palo Alto, California

“You can’t connect the dots looking forward; you can only connect them looking backward. So you have to trust that the dots will somehow connect in your future. You have to trust in something — your gut, destiny, life, karma, whatever.”

-- Steve Jobs
